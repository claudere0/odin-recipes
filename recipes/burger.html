Burger recipe

Home

Ingredients

  • For the Patties:

    • 1 lb (450g) ground chicken (a mix of white and dark meat is juicier)
    • ¼ cup plain breadcrumbs
    • ½ small onion, finely minced or grated
    • 1 large egg, lightly beaten (acts as a binder)
    • 2 cloves garlic, minced (or ½ tsp garlic powder)
    • ½ tsp salt and ¼ tsp black pepper, to taste
    • 1 Tbsp olive or vegetable oil (for cooking)

  • For Assembly:

    • 4 hamburger buns, split
    • Mayonnaise, ketchup, or a preferred sauce
    • Lettuce leaves, tomato and onion slices (optional)
    • 4 slices cheese (optional)

Description

  1. Prepare the Mixture

    In a large bowl, combine the ground chicken, breadcrumbs, minced onion, beaten egg, minced garlic, salt, and pepper. Mix until just combined, being careful not to overmix, which can make the patties tough.

  2. Shape the Patties

    Divide the mixture into 4 equal portions and shape them into patties, about ½ to ¾ inch thick. Press a shallow indent into the center of each with your thumb to help them cook evenly and prevent puffing up.

  3. Cook the Patties

    Heat the oil in a large skillet or a grill pan over medium-high heat. Place the chicken patties in the hot pan and cook for 4-6 minutes per side, until golden brown and cooked through. The internal temperature should reach 165°F (74°C). If adding cheese, place a slice on top of each patty during the last minute of cooking and cover briefly to melt.

  4. Toast the Buns

    While the patties cook, lightly toast the hamburger buns on the grill or in a separate pan until golden and warm.

  5. Assemble the Burger

    Spread your chosen sauce (mayo, ketchup, etc.) on the bottom half of each toasted bun. Add a layer of lettuce and tomato slices (if using), then top with a chicken patty. Place the top bun on and serve immediately.
