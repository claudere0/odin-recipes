Nuggets recipe

Home

Ingredients

  • 1 lb (450g) boneless, skinless chicken breasts, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • ½ cup all-purpose flour
  • 2 large eggs, lightly beaten
  • 1 cup breadcrumbs (panko works well for extra crunch)
  • ½ tsp salt and ¼ tsp black pepper, or to taste
  • Vegetable or olive oil for pan-frying/baking

Description

  1. First, you will need 1 lb (450g) of boneless, skinless chicken breasts cut into bite-sized pieces, ½ cup of all-purpose flour, 2 large eggs lightly beaten, 1 cup of breadcrumbs (panko is a good choice for crispiness), ½ tsp of salt, ¼ tsp of black pepper, and vegetable or olive oil for cooking.

  2. To start the process, set up a simple breading station using three shallow bowls. In the first bowl, combine the flour with salt and pepper. In the second bowl, place the beaten eggs. In the third bowl, add your breadcrumbs. You may add extra seasonings like paprika or garlic powder to the breadcrumbs at this stage if you wish.

  3. Next, coat the chicken pieces one by one. Dip each piece into the flour first, shaking off any excess. Then, dip it into the egg mixture, allowing the excess to drip off. Finally, coat the piece thoroughly in the breadcrumbs, pressing gently to ensure they adhere well to the chicken.

  4. Once all the chicken pieces are coated, you can choose your cooking method. If you prefer to pan-fry, heat about half an inch of oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Carefully add the nuggets in batches to avoid overcrowding the pan, and fry for 3 to 4 minutes per side, until golden brown and fully cooked through.

  5. Alternatively, you can bake them. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C) and place the breaded chicken pieces on a lined baking sheet. Bake for 15 to 20 minutes, making sure to flip them halfway through, until they are golden and the chicken is fully cooked internally.

  6. Finally, transfer the cooked nuggets to a paper towel-lined plate to drain any excess oil (if fried) and serve them warm with your favorite dipping sauces like ketchup, barbecue sauce, or honey mustard.
