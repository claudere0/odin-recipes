Pizza recipe

Home

Ingredients

  • warm water
  • granulated sugar
  • instant yeast
  • all-purpose flour
  • extra-virgin olive oil
  • salt

Description

  1. Prepare the Dough

    The dough is made by mixing flour, yeast, water, salt, and olive oil, then kneading it until it's smooth and elastic. The dough is then covered and set aside to rise in a warm place until it has doubled in size, which usually takes about 45 minutes to an hour and a half, depending on the temperature and type of yeast.

    • Tip: You can also buy pre-made dough from a grocery store for a quicker option.

  2. Preheat the Oven

    For a crispy crust, preheat your oven to the highest possible temperature (around 450-550°F or 230-290°C) for at least 30 minutes, preferably with a pizza stone or an upturned heavy baking sheet inside. High heat is key to achieving a good texture.

  3. Shape the Dough

    Once risen, punch the dough down to release the air, and divide it into portions (one pound of dough usually makes two 10-12 inch pizzas). On a lightly floured surface, use your hands or a rolling pin to flatten and stretch the dough into a circle, leaving the edges slightly thicker for the crust.

  4. Add Toppings

    Transfer the shaped dough to a pizza peel or baking sheet dusted with cornmeal or flour. Spread a thin layer of sauce evenly over the base, leaving a small border for the crust. Add your desired toppings and sprinkle with cheese.

    • Tip: Avoid overloading the pizza with too many toppings, as this can make the crust soggy.

  5. Bake the Pizza

    Carefully slide the pizza onto the preheated stone or baking sheet in the oven. Bake for 8–15 minutes, or until the crust is golden brown and the cheese is melted and bubbling.

  6. Serve

    Remove the pizza from the oven, let it cool for a few minutes, then slice and enjoy.
